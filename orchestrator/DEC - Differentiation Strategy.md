# DEC — Differentiation Strategy

## The Problem

Our platform model is **functional but undifferentiated**. iProperty, Mudah, and uni portals offer the same core utility. We need a real reason for students and landlords to choose Ten&See.

## Options

| Option | Description | Effort | Risk | Reward |
|--------|-------------|--------|------|--------|
| **A. Tech-First** | AI matching, AR tours, predictive pricing | High | High (expensive, may not solve real pain) | Very High (scalable, defensible) |
| **B. Service-First** | High-touch concierge: we place students, not just list | Medium | Medium (labor-intensive) | High (hard to replicate, premium fees) |
| **C. Niche Domination** | Focus: international students only, or postgrads only | Low | Low (small TAM) | Medium (strong word-of-mouth) |
| **D. Vertical Integration** | We lease and sublet properties ourselves | Very High | Very High (capital, ops complexity) | Very High (full control, margins) |

## Current Lean

**B. Service-First** — moving from self-serve to student housing placement service.

## What We Need to Decide

1. **Which option?** (A, B, C, D, or hybrid)
2. **What does the MVP look like?**
3. **What does the team need to change?**
4. **What metrics prove it works?**
